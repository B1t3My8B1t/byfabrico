Privacy Policy

Last updated: April 2026

Who We Are

This privacy policy applies to Fabrico Design & Build Ltd, a construction and interior design company based in London. Our registered address is 54 Poland Street, Soho, London W1F 7NJ.

If you have any questions about how we handle your data, contact us at info@byfabrico.com.

What Data We Collect

We only collect personal data that you provide to us directly via the enquiry form on this website. This may include:

  • Your name
  • Email address
  • Phone number
  • Project location and budget range
  • Description of your project
  • Services you are interested in

We do not collect any data passively. This website uses no cookies, tracking scripts, or analytics tools.

How We Use Your Data

We use the information you submit solely to respond to your enquiry and to discuss your project with you. We will not use your details for marketing, add you to any mailing list, or share your information with third parties for commercial purposes.

Data Retention

Enquiry emails are retained for one month, after which they are permanently deleted. We do not store your data in any database or CRM system.

Third Parties

Your enquiry form submission is processed by Web3Forms, a third-party form handling service, which transmits your data to our inbox. Web3Forms acts as a data processor on our behalf and does not retain your data beyond the transmission. You can review their privacy policy at web3forms.com/privacy.

This website loads fonts from Google Fonts. This causes your browser to make a request to Google's servers, which may log your IP address as part of standard server operation. No personal data is shared with Google by us.

Cookies

This website does not use cookies.

Your Rights

Under UK GDPR, you have the right to:

  • Request a copy of any personal data we hold about you
  • Request that we correct or delete your personal data
  • Object to or restrict how we process your data

To exercise any of these rights, contact us at info@byfabrico.com. We will respond within 30 days.

You also have the right to lodge a complaint with the Information Commissioner's Office (ICO) if you believe your data has been mishandled.

Changes to This Policy

We may update this policy from time to time. The date at the top of this page reflects when it was last revised. Continued use of this website following any changes constitutes acceptance of the updated policy.
